MIRCHI-KA-SALAN


INGREDIENTS

CHILLIS

10

WATER

2 CUPS

TURMERIC POWDER

1 tbsp

GARLIC

2 tbsp

GREEN CHILIES

2

SALT

2 tbsp

CILANTRO

2 ROUGHLY CHOPPED

OIL

2 tbsp

ASAFOETIDA

1/4 tbsp

MUSTARD-CUMIN

2 tbsp

CURRY LEAVES

2 tbsp

STEPS

(60 MINUTES)

  • Wash 8-10 large green chilies, slit them lengthwise, and remove the seeds if you prefer less heat. Keep the stems intact for presentation.
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a heavy-bottomed pan and fry the chilies on medium heat until they blister and turn light brown. Remove the chilies from the pan and set them aside.
  • In the same pan, dry roast 2 tablespoons of peanuts, 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds, and 1 tablespoon of grated coconut until golden and aromatic. Set these aside to cool.
  • Blend the roasted peanuts, sesame seeds, coconut, 1 roughly chopped onion and 2 teaspoons of ginger-garlic paste into a smooth paste. Add a little water if needed.
  • Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in the same pan. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds, 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, and 1/2 teaspoon of fenugreek seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Add 10 curry leaves and sauté for a few seconds, then add the ground masala paste. Sauté on medium heat until the oil starts to separate from the paste.
  • Add 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste. Stir and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  • Add 1 tablespoon of tamarind pulp and 1/2 cup of water. Mix well and simmer the gravy for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Add the fried green chilies to the simmering gravy and cook for an additional 5 minutes, allowing the chilies to absorb the flavors.
  • Turn off the heat and garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with biryani for a delicious meal.

Mirchi Ka Salan has a rich, nutty flavor that comes from the roasted peanuts, sesame seeds, and coconut used in the masala paste. The fried green chilies absorb the tangy, spiced tamarind gravy, offering a delightful balance of smoky heat and creamy texture, making it a unique and flavorful curry.

This dish combines the earthy taste of roasted spices and the tang of tamarind to create a gravy that's both mildly spiced and slightly tangy. The tender, blistered green chilies in this gravy make Mirchi Ka Salan a perfect accompaniment to biryani, adding a complex flavor to a festive meal.
