PANEER CURRY


INGREDIENTS

PANEER

1 1/2 KG

CURRY LEAVES

2 STEMS

GINGER

GARLIC

2 tbsp

MUSTARD

SEEDS

2 tbsp

ONIONS

2 ROUGHLY CHOPPED

TOMATOES

4 LARGE SLICED

CARDAMON

3

PEPPER

4

CLOVE

4

CUMIN

1 tbsp

TAMARIND

20 GMS SOAKED

CORAINDER

2 STEMS

STEPS

(40 MINUTES)

  • First, cut 200g of paneer into cubes and set aside.
  • In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat 1 tablespoon of oil on medium heat.
  • Once the oil is hot, add the paneer cubes and lightly fry them until they are golden on all sides. Remove the fried paneer and keep it aside.
  • In the same pan, add 1 more tablespoon of oil and let it heat up
  • Once the oil is hot, add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them crackle for a few seconds.
  • Next, add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until it turns golden brown.
  • Add 1 teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  • Now, add 2 pureed tomatoes and cook the mixture on medium heat for about 5-7 minutes until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  • Add 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, 1 teaspoon of cumin powder, 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala, and 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder. Mix everything well and cook for 2 minutes.
  • Add 1/2 cup of water and salt to taste. Stir well and let it simmer for 5 minutes.
  • After 5 minutes, gently add the fried paneer cubes into the curry. Stir well to coat the paneer in the curry.
  • Crush 1/2 teaspoon of kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) between your palms and add it to the curry along with 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala.
  • Let the paneer simmer in the curry for 3-4 minutes, so it absorbs all the flavors.
  • If you prefer a richer curry, add 1/4 cup of cream or milk at this stage and mix well. Let it cook for another 2 minutes.
  • Finally, turn off the flame and garnish the paneer curry with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.
  • Serve Hot

Paneer Curry has a rich, creamy texture and a mild, spiced flavor that comes from the addition of fried paneer and a perfect blend of Indian spices. The light frying of paneer creates a slightly crisp exterior, making each bite both soft and flavorful, complementing the smooth curry.

This curry, with its subtle notes of kasuri methi and garam masala, offers a perfect balance of earthy and aromatic flavors, making it a popular choice in North Indian cuisine. It's a comforting dish often served at family gatherings, best paired with soft naan or fragrant basmati rice for a satisfying meal.
